**An Open Research platform for Computational Mechanics, Material Characterisation and Physics-Guided Machine Learning**

**Introduction**
Modern structural materials generate enormous data with respect to experiments and simulation, but computational workflows, which are reproducible, for analysing fatigue, fracture, creep and micrstructure-property relationships are still fragmented across various research groups. **mechAiLab** aims to bridge this gap by providing an open source, reproducible research platform for computational mechanics and material science. This project combines classical mechanics, data driven modelling and modern ML into one framework for studying the mechanical behaviour of structual materials.
Rather than trying to replace established mechanics theories, mechAiLab investigates how AI can complement physics-based models to improve prediction, interpretation and understanding of material behaviour.

**Research Themes**

This repo is organised around five interconnected research areas:

**1. Fatigue**:
S-N curve analysis
Basquin models
Coffin-Manson models
Rainflow counting
RUL prediction
Physics guided fatigue modelling

**2. Fracture Mechanics**
Paris Law
Crack growth Prediction
Fracture toughness datasets
Damage accumulation
Crack propagation modelling

**3. Creep and High Temperature Deformation**
Time-dependent deformation
Stress relaxation
Larson-Miller parameter analysis
Creep life prediction
High temperature alloy datasets

**4. Microstructure Property Relationships**
Grain size analysis
EBSD-derived descriptors
Texture Analysis
Phase fraction prediction
Structure-property modelling
GNNs

**5. Damage Characterization**
Acoustic Emission
Load-displacement correlation
Digital image Correlation
Sequential damage evolution
Failure mode identification
Uncertainty estimation

**Core Objectives**

Build an Open Mechanics Dataset Library covering composites, metals, polymers, ceramics, aerospace materials, fatigue, fracture, creep, tensile testing, AE etc.
Benchmark Classical Mechanics against ML
Develop Open Educational Resources which will include tutorial notebooks explaining fracture mechanics, fatigue, ML, GNN, AI alongside research code
